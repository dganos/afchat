# Road Types in Germany

## Classification of Roads

German roads are classified into several categories, each with different rules and characteristics.

## 1. Autobahn (Federal Motorway)

- Designated by the letter **"A"** followed by a number (e.g., A1, A7, A9)
- Signs are **blue** with white text
- Divided highway with at least 2 lanes per direction, separated by a median
- No traffic lights, no intersections, no pedestrian crossings
- Access only via designated on-ramps (Auffahrten)
- Total network: approximately **13,200 km** (one of the densest motorway networks globally)
- Maintained by the federal government (Autobahn GmbH)
- Speed: No general limit (130 km/h advisory)

## 2. Bundesstraße (Federal Road)

- Designated by the letter **"B"** followed by a number (e.g., B1, B27, B96)
- Signs are **yellow** with black text
- Can be single carriageway or dual carriageway
- May pass through towns and villages
- Speed limit: 100 km/h outside urban areas (50 km/h within)
- Often serves as the main connection between cities where no Autobahn exists
- Total network: approximately **37,800 km**

## 3. Landesstraße / Staatsstraße (State Road)

- Designated by the letter **"L"** or **"S"** depending on the federal state
- Maintained by the respective Bundesland (federal state)
- Connects smaller towns and links them to Bundesstraßen and Autobahn
- Speed limit: Same as general rules (100 km/h outside towns, 50 km/h inside)
- Road quality varies by state and region

## 4. Kreisstraße (County/District Road)

- Designated by the letter **"K"**
- Maintained by the Landkreis (county/district)
- Connects villages and local communities
- Often narrower, sometimes without center markings
- Speed limit: General rules apply

## 5. Gemeindestraße (Municipal Road)

- Local roads within cities, towns, and villages
- No standardized numbering
- Includes residential streets, access roads, and inner-city routes
- Speed limit: 50 km/h default, often 30 km/h zones

## 6. Spielstraße / Verkehrsberuhigter Bereich (Traffic-Calmed Area)

- Marked by sign 325.1 (blue sign with house, car, pedestrian, and child)
- Maximum speed: **walking pace (Schrittgeschwindigkeit)** — approx. 4–7 km/h
- Pedestrians may use the full width of the road
- Children may play on the road
- Parking is only allowed in designated spaces

## 7. Feldweg / Waldweg (Agricultural/Forest Roads)

- Unpaved or partially paved roads in rural areas
- Access may be restricted to agricultural or forestry vehicles
- Signs typically indicate "Anlieger frei" (residents/authorized vehicles only)
- Not intended for through traffic

## Road Numbering System

| Type | Prefix | Sign Color | Example |
|---|---|---|---|
| Autobahn | A | Blue | A7 |
| Bundesstraße | B | Yellow | B27 |
| Landesstraße | L/S | White/Yellow | L1100 |
| Kreisstraße | K | White | K5020 |
| Europastraße | E | Green | E45 |

## Europastraßen (European Routes)
- Part of the international E-road network
- Designated by **"E"** with green signs
- Overlap with existing Autobahn or Bundesstraße routes
- Example: E45 runs from Gothenburg (Sweden) through Hamburg, Hannover, Munich to Southern Italy
