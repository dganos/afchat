# Velocity V-Fast LSA — Pilot Operating Handbook

*Document number: AR-POH-015  ·  Revision: 4.2  ·  Effective: 2026-01-15*

The Velocity V-Fast LSA is a light sport aircraft used by training, commercial, and
research operators. This manual covers normal and emergency operating
procedures, weight-and-balance limits, and the minimum equipment list
sufficient for daylight VFR dispatch. Crews must hold a current type
rating on the Velocity and complete a yearly recurrency check.

## 2. Pre-flight Inspection

The pre-flight check is mandatory before every departure regardless of
the time since the previous flight. Discrepancies must be entered in the
aircraft technical log.

1. Walk-around: control surface hinges, no fuel/oil staining around cowl
2. Pitot–static system covers removed, drains clear
3. Fuel sumps drained at all low points; sample colour and clarity checked
4. Tyre pressure and strut extension within limits
5. Trim tabs neutral and free; flap retraction confirmed
6. Propeller spinner secure, blade leading edges undamaged
7. Engine oil within green arc, dipstick re-seated
8. Static ports unobstructed and pitot heat operative
9. Brake reservoir level and parking brake function checked
10. Stall warning vane free, audible warning operative

## 3. Normal Procedures

### 3.1 Start-up

1. Master switch ON, avionics OFF.
2. Fuel selector to the fullest tank (single-engine) or BOTH (twin).
3. Mixture rich (piston) / condition lever IDLE (turbine).
4. Throttle cracked 1 cm; pre-rotate / motor as applicable.
5. Confirm start within 30 seconds; abort if ITT/CHT exceeds limits.

### 3.2 Take-off

1. Cleared for take-off — align with runway centerline.
2. Apply power smoothly to full throttle, target 92% N1 or 2700 RPM.
3. Rotate / lift at 66 KIAS.
4. Climb attitude 8–10° nose up; positive rate, gear up if equipped.
5. Reduce to climb power at 400 ft AGL.

### 3.3 Cruise

Trim for level flight at cruise altitude. Monitor engine instruments
every 10 minutes; fuel flow should remain within ±5% of planned.
Avoid the vibration band at 1850–1950 RPM for sustained operation.

### 3.4 Landing

Reduce power at the IAF. Configure for landing on base leg. Maintain
38 KIAS on final; touchdown target is the first third of the
runway / landing zone. Apply braking smoothly to taxi speed.

## 4. Emergency Procedures

### 4.1 Engine failure after take-off

1. Lower the nose immediately to maintain best-glide airspeed
2. Fuel pump ON, mixture rich, magnetos BOTH, fuel selector to fullest tank
3. If no restart by 300 ft AGL: commit to land within ±30° of nose
4. Master switch off short final; door cracked open
5. Mayday on 121.5; squawk 7700

### 4.2 Electrical smoke or fire

1. Master switch OFF immediately
2. Cabin vents open; cabin heat OFF
3. Land as soon as practical; non-essential equipment OFF
4. If smoke persists with master off, suspect cabin source — locate and isolate

### 4.3 Loss of pressurisation

1. Don oxygen masks, 100% setting
2. Emergency descent: throttle idle, speed brake out, target 10,000 ft
3. Notify ATC, request lower altitude
4. Check passenger oxygen deployed and flow indicators green


## 5. Aircraft-Specific Cautions

- Coolant temperature ≥ 50 °C required before take-off run-up

## 6. Weight and Balance

Empty weight is recorded on the aircraft equipment list. Pilots must
calculate take-off and landing weights, and verify the centre of
gravity falls within the published envelope. Operating outside the
envelope is prohibited.

## 7. Minimum Equipment

For VFR-day dispatch the following must be operative:

- Primary attitude indicator
- Altimeter (Kollsman-adjustable)
- Airspeed indicator
- Magnetic compass
- Engine RPM/torque indication
- Fuel quantity indicator (each tank)
- Communications radio (one operative)

## 8. Maintenance Schedule

- **50 hr** — oil change, filter inspection, control rigging check
- **100 hr** — full airframe inspection, magneto timing
- **300 hr** — gearbox/transmission filter, blade tracking
- **Annual** — comprehensive airworthiness inspection
- **Calendar** — pitot–static and transponder check every 24 months
