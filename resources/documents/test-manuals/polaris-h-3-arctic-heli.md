# Polaris H-3 Arctic — Pilot Operating Handbook

*Document number: AR-POH-007  ·  Revision: 4.2  ·  Effective: 2026-01-15*

The Polaris H-3 Arctic is a cold-weather operations aircraft used by training, commercial, and
research operators. This manual covers normal and emergency operating
procedures, weight-and-balance limits, and the minimum equipment list
sufficient for daylight VFR dispatch. Crews must hold a current type
rating on the Polaris and complete a yearly recurrency check.

## 2. Pre-flight Inspection

The pre-flight check is mandatory before every departure regardless of
the time since the previous flight. Discrepancies must be entered in the
aircraft technical log.

1. Walk-around: blade condition, droop stops free, no Jesus-nut play
2. Tail rotor: check pitch links, control rod, no slop in pedal feedback
3. Engine cowling secured, oil quantity within green band
4. Fuel sample drained for water/sediment from sump and tank low point
5. Mast and swashplate visually inspected for cracks or oil weep
6. Battery voltage ≥ 24 V before start; ground power off
7. Skid/gear shock absorbers extended, no fluid leakage on collars
8. Pitot heat checked operative; static ports clear
9. Doors, latches, and cargo nets secured
10. Cyclic, collective, and pedals free and correct travel

## 3. Normal Procedures

### 3.1 Start-up

1. Master switch ON, avionics OFF.
2. Fuel selector to the fullest tank (single-engine) or BOTH (twin).
3. Mixture rich (piston) / condition lever IDLE (turbine).
4. Throttle cracked 1 cm; pre-rotate / motor as applicable.
5. Confirm start within 30 seconds; abort if ITT/CHT exceeds limits.

### 3.2 Take-off

1. Cleared for take-off — align with runway centerline.
2. Apply power smoothly to max continuous torque.
3. Rotate / lift at ETL (effective translational lift).
4. Climb attitude 5–7° nose up; positive rate, gear up if equipped.
5. Reduce to climb power at 400 ft AGL.

### 3.3 Cruise

Trim for level flight at cruise altitude. Monitor engine instruments
every 10 minutes; fuel flow should remain within ±5% of planned.
Avoid the vibration band at 65–72% Nr for sustained operation.

### 3.4 Landing

Reduce power at the IAF. Configure for landing on base leg. Maintain
62 KIAS on final; touchdown target is the first third of the
runway / landing zone. Apply braking smoothly to taxi speed.

## 4. Emergency Procedures

### 4.1 Engine failure in cruise

1. Lower collective immediately to maintain rotor RPM in green band
2. Establish 60–70 KIAS for best autorotation glide
3. Select landing area into wind, avoid wires and slopes > 5°
4. Mayday on 121.5; squawk 7700
5. Flare at 75 ft, level at 15 ft, cushion with collective

### 4.2 Tail rotor drive failure

1. Immediately reduce power; close throttle on piston ships
2. Lower collective to keep yaw rate manageable
3. Establish forward airspeed for streamlining (min 45 KIAS)
4. Plan run-on landing into wind on a hard surface
5. Touch down with minimum power, full down collective at slide stop

### 4.3 Engine fire on the ground

1. Throttle to idle cut-off, fuel valve OFF
2. Battery and generator OFF
3. Evacuate via upwind door; use fire extinguisher only after evac if safe
4. Notify ATC and ground crew


## 5. Aircraft-Specific Cautions

- Engine inlet anti-ice mandatory below 4 °C OAT in visible moisture
- Battery heat blanket required for cold-soak below −20 °C

## 6. Weight and Balance

Empty weight is recorded on the aircraft equipment list. Pilots must
calculate take-off and landing weights, and verify the centre of
gravity falls within the published envelope. Operating outside the
envelope is prohibited.

## 7. Minimum Equipment

For VFR-day dispatch the following must be operative:

- Primary attitude indicator
- Altimeter (Kollsman-adjustable)
- Airspeed indicator
- Magnetic compass
- Engine RPM/torque indication
- Fuel quantity indicator (each tank)
- Communications radio (one operative)

## 8. Maintenance Schedule

- **50 hr** — oil change, filter inspection, control rigging check
- **100 hr** — full airframe inspection, magneto timing
- **300 hr** — gearbox/transmission filter, blade tracking
- **Annual** — comprehensive airworthiness inspection
- **Calendar** — pitot–static and transponder check every 24 months
