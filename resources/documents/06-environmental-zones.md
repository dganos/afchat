# Environmental Zones (Umweltzonen) in Germany

## Overview

Germany operates Low Emission Zones (Umweltzonen) in many cities to reduce air pollution. Vehicles must display the correct emission sticker (Umweltplakette/Feinstaubplakette) to enter these zones.

## Emission Sticker Categories

| Sticker Color | Euro Standard | Allowed in Umweltzonen |
|---|---|---|
| No sticker | Euro 1 or older | Not allowed |
| Red (Schadstoffgruppe 2) | Euro 2 | Very few zones |
| Yellow (Schadstoffgruppe 3) | Euro 3 | Some zones |
| **Green (Schadstoffgruppe 4)** | **Euro 4, 5, 6** | **All zones** |

**As of 2024, almost all Umweltzonen in Germany require the green sticker (Euro 4+).**

## How to Get a Sticker

- Purchase from: Vehicle registration offices (Zulassungsstelle), TÜV/DEKRA stations, authorized repair shops (AU-Werkstätten)
- Cost: **€5–€15**
- Required documents: Vehicle registration certificate (Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I)
- The sticker is tied to the **license plate number** and must be affixed to the **lower right of the windshield**
- Foreign vehicles also need the sticker — available online or at border-area service points

## Cities with Environmental Zones

More than 50 German cities have Umweltzonen, including:
- Berlin (largest zone, covers most of the inner city within the S-Bahn ring)
- Munich
- Stuttgart (strictest regulations in Germany)
- Cologne
- Frankfurt am Main
- Düsseldorf
- Hamburg (specific diesel driving restrictions on certain streets)
- Hannover
- Leipzig
- Dortmund
- Essen

## Penalties for Violations

- Driving in an Umweltzone without the correct sticker: **€100**
- No points are assigned for this violation
- Foreign vehicles are not exempt — the same rules apply

## Diesel Driving Bans

In addition to Umweltzonen, some cities have implemented specific diesel driving bans:
- **Stuttgart**: Euro 4 diesel vehicles banned from the entire city area; Euro 5 diesel banned from certain streets
- **Hamburg**: Two street sections banned for Euro 5 diesel trucks and older diesel cars
- **Berlin**: Parts of a major highway (A100) have discussed diesel restrictions
- These bans apply to specific streets and are marked with special signage

## Electric and Low-Emission Vehicles

- Electric vehicles (EVs) may receive special privileges:
  - Free parking in some cities
  - Use of bus lanes (where permitted by local rules)
  - Exemption from certain access restrictions
- The **E-Kennzeichen** (license plate ending in "E") identifies electric and plug-in hybrid vehicles
- Plug-in hybrids qualify only if they have a minimum electric range of 40 km or emit less than 50g CO₂/km
