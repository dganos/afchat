# Vehicle Requirements and Inspections in Germany

## Mandatory Vehicle Equipment

Every vehicle driven on German roads must carry or be equipped with:

### Required by Law
1. **Warning triangle (Warndreieck)**: Must be easily accessible, not buried in the trunk
2. **First aid kit (Verbandskasten)**: Must conform to DIN 13164 standard and not be expired
3. **Reflective safety vest (Warnweste)**: At least one per vehicle (EN ISO 20471 certified)
   - Must be inside the passenger cabin, not in the trunk
   - Recommended: one vest per seat
4. **Valid registration (Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil I)**: Must be carried while driving
5. **Valid insurance (Haftpflichtversicherung)**: Third-party liability insurance is mandatory
6. **License plates (Kennzeichen)**: Front and rear, properly mounted and readable
7. **Environmental sticker (Umweltplakette)**: Required for entering Umweltzonen

### Seasonal Requirements
- **Winter tires (Winterreifen)**: Mandatory in winter conditions (M+S or 3PMSF marked)
  - Required when roads are icy, snowy, slushy, or covered in frost
  - No fixed date mandate — it's **situational** ("bei winterlichen Straßenverhältnissen")
  - Common advice: "Von O bis O" (October to Easter/Ostern)
  - Minimum tread depth: **1.6 mm legal minimum**, but **4 mm recommended** for winter tires
  - Fine for driving without proper tires in winter conditions: **€60 + 1 point**
  - If causing an obstruction: **€80 + 1 point**

### Tire Regulations
- Minimum tread depth: **1.6 mm** (legal minimum for all tires)
- Tires must match the specifications in the vehicle registration
- Mixing tire types (summer/winter) on the same axle is prohibited
- Snow chains: permitted on certain roads, mandatory when sign 268 is posted

## TÜV (Hauptuntersuchung — HU)

The TÜV inspection (technically "Hauptuntersuchung") is Germany's mandatory vehicle safety and emissions inspection.

### Frequency
- New vehicles: First inspection after **3 years**
- After that: Every **2 years**
- Taxis: Every **12 months**
- Vehicles used for passenger transport: Annual

### What is Inspected
- Brakes (function and wear)
- Steering and suspension
- Lights and electrical systems
- Tires (condition and tread depth)
- Exhaust and emissions (Abgasuntersuchung — AU)
- Chassis and body (rust, structural integrity)
- Windshield and wipers
- Horn
- Seat belts
- Mirrors

### Results
- **Keine Mängel (No defects)**: Pass — new TÜV sticker issued
- **Geringe Mängel (Minor defects)**: Pass — defects should be repaired soon
- **Erhebliche Mängel (Significant defects)**: Fail — must be repaired and re-inspected within 4 weeks
- **Gefährliche Mängel (Dangerous defects)**: Fail — vehicle may not be driven until repaired
- **Verkehrsunsicher (Unsafe for traffic)**: Immediate prohibition of use

### TÜV Sticker
- Colored round sticker on the rear license plate
- Color indicates the year of the next inspection
- The number at the top of the sticker indicates the month
- Overdue TÜV fines:
  - 2–4 months overdue: **€15**
  - 4–8 months overdue: **€25**
  - More than 8 months overdue: **€60 + 1 point**

### Cost
- Standard HU + AU: approximately **€100–€150**
- Conducted by: TÜV, DEKRA, GTÜ, KÜS, or authorized inspection stations

## Vehicle Registration

### New Registration (Zulassung)
Required documents:
- Proof of identity (Personalausweis or Reisepass)
- Vehicle registration certificate Part II (Fahrzeugbrief / Zulassungsbescheinigung Teil II)
- Proof of insurance (eVB-Nummer — electronic insurance confirmation)
- SEPA mandate for vehicle tax (Kfz-Steuer)
- Valid HU report (for used vehicles)
- Cost: approximately **€26–€30** for plates + registration fees

### De-Registration (Abmeldung)
- Required when selling, scrapping, or exporting a vehicle
- Bring both parts of the registration certificate + license plates
- Plates are invalidated (stamped through)
