# Traffic Signs in Germany

## Overview

German traffic signs are standardized and follow European conventions. They are categorized into several groups based on their function.

## 1. Warning Signs (Gefahrzeichen)

Triangular signs with a red border on a white background. They warn of hazards ahead.

| Sign Number | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 101 | General danger (with supplementary sign specifying the hazard) |
| 102 | Crossroads ahead (intersection with right-of-way road) |
| 103 | Curve ahead (left or right) |
| 105 | Double curve ahead |
| 108 | Steep gradient (downhill) |
| 110 | Steep incline (uphill) |
| 112 | Uneven road surface |
| 114 | Slippery road (especially when wet or icy) |
| 117 | Lateral wind / crosswind |
| 120 | Narrow road ahead |
| 123 | Construction zone ahead (Arbeitsstelle) |
| 124 | Traffic jam likely (Stau) |
| 131 | Light signal ahead (traffic light) |
| 133 | Pedestrian crossing ahead |
| 136 | Children (near schools or playgrounds) |
| 142 | Animal crossing (usually deer) |

## 4. Information Signs (Richtzeichen)

Rectangular or square signs providing guidance and information.

| Sign Number | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 310 | Town name (start of urban area — 50 km/h applies) |

## 5. Supplementary Signs (Zusatzzeichen)

Small rectangular signs placed below main signs to add conditions or details.

Common supplementary signs:
- **"bei Nässe"** — "when wet" (e.g., speed limit applies only when road is wet)
- **"Mo–Fr 7–18h"** — time restriction
- **"Anlieger frei"** — local traffic exempt
- **"Radfahrer frei"** — cyclists exempt
- **Distance plates** — indicate distance to the hazard or start of the restriction
- **Arrow plates** — show the extent/direction of a restriction (e.g., no parking from here)

## 6. Temporary Signs (Construction and Detour)

- Temporary signs have a **yellow background** instead of white
- They take precedence over permanent signs
- Common in construction zones (Baustellen)
- Include temporary speed limits, lane changes, and detour routes (Umleitung)

## Key Rules About Signs
- When temporary and permanent signs conflict, **temporary signs take precedence**
- Sign 274 (speed limit) remains in effect until explicitly cancelled by sign 278 (end of speed limit) or by an intersection in some cases
- Town entry sign (310) automatically sets the speed limit to **50 km/h**
- Town exit sign (311) automatically sets the speed limit to **100 km/h** (unless otherwise posted)
- Electronic variable signs (Wechselverkehrszeichen) on the Autobahn take precedence over static signs
