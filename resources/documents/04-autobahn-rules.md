# Autobahn Rules and Regulations

## Access Requirements

The German Autobahn may only be used by vehicles that:
- Are capable of reaching at least **60 km/h** on flat ground
- Are permitted to drive on public roads (registered, insured, roadworthy)

The following are **not allowed** on the Autobahn:
- Pedestrians
- Cyclists
- Mopeds and small scooters (under 60 km/h)
- Agricultural vehicles and tractors
- Horse-drawn vehicles

## Driving Rules

### Right-Lane Driving (Rechtsfahrgebot)
- Drivers must use the **rightmost available lane** (§2 StVO)
- The left lane(s) are for overtaking only
- After overtaking, you must return to the right lane
- "Lane hogging" (Mittelspurschleichen) in the middle lane is a fineable offense: **€80 + 1 point**

### Overtaking
- Overtaking on the **right is prohibited** (Rechtsüberholen) on the Autobahn
- Exception: In congested traffic (Stau) where all lanes are moving slowly, the right lane may move faster
- When passing on the right is unavoidable in slow traffic, the speed difference must not exceed **20 km/h**
- Trucks over 3.5 tons: overtaking ban applies on many stretches, especially two-lane Autobahn sections

### Minimum Following Distance
- The recommended following distance is **half the speedometer reading in meters**
- Example: At 130 km/h, keep at least 65 meters distance
- Tailgating fines range from **€75 to €400** depending on speed and distance, with up to 2 points and driving bans

### Emergency Corridor (Rettungsgasse)
- **Mandatory** when traffic comes to a standstill or moves very slowly
- Vehicles in the **left lane** move to the **left**
- Vehicles in **all other lanes** move to the **right**
- This creates a corridor between the leftmost lane and the next lane
- Failure to form a Rettungsgasse: **€200–€320 + 2 points + 1 month driving ban**

## Breakdown and Emergency Procedures

1. Turn on hazard lights immediately
2. Put on your **reflective safety vest** (Warnweste) before exiting the vehicle
3. Exit on the side away from traffic (right side)
4. Place the **warning triangle** at least **200 meters** behind your vehicle on the Autobahn
5. Call for assistance: **ADAC (tel: 0800-5 10 11 12)** or emergency services **(112)**
6. Wait behind the crash barrier, never on the road or shoulder

## Rest Areas and Services

### Rastplätze (Rest areas)
- Simple rest stops with parking, sometimes picnic tables and toilets
- No fuel or food services
- Marked with the "P" sign

### Raststätten (Service areas)
- Full service: fuel, restaurants, toilets, shops
- Open 24/7 in most cases
- Operated by companies like Tank & Rast
- Toilet use typically costs €0.70 (Sanifair system, with €0.50 voucher for purchases)

### Autohöfe
- Located near Autobahn exits (not directly on the Autobahn)
- Often cheaper fuel and food than Raststätten
- Popular with truck drivers

## Toll Information
- As of 2024, there is **no car toll** (PKW-Maut) on German Autobahns
- Trucks over 7.5 tons must pay toll via the **Toll Collect** system (GPS-based)
- Truck toll rates depend on emission class, weight, and number of axles
