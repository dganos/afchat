# Public Transportation Rules in Germany

## Overview

Germany has an extensive public transportation network including trains, buses, trams, and subways. The system is operated by various regional and national providers.

## Major Operators

### Long-Distance
- **Deutsche Bahn (DB)**: National rail operator, operates ICE (high-speed), IC/EC (intercity), and regional trains
- **FlixTrain**: Private competitor on select long-distance routes
- **FlixBus**: Major long-distance bus operator

### Regional and Local
- Each region has its own Verkehrsverbund (transport association):
  - **VBB** (Berlin-Brandenburg)
  - **MVV** (Munich)
  - **VRS** (Cologne/Bonn)
  - **VVS** (Stuttgart)
  - **HVV** (Hamburg)
  - **RMV** (Frankfurt/Rhine-Main)

## Ticket Types

### Single Tickets (Einzelfahrschein)
- Valid for one journey within a specific zone
- Prices vary by city and zone: typically **€2.50–€4.00** for urban journeys

### Day Tickets (Tageskarte)
- Unlimited travel within selected zones for one day
- Typically **€7–€15** depending on the city and zone coverage
- Group day tickets available for up to 5 people

### Weekly/Monthly Passes (Wochenkarte/Monatskarte)
- Unlimited travel for the validity period
- Monthly passes: **€60–€120** depending on the city

### Deutschlandticket (49-Euro-Ticket)
- Introduced in May 2023, price increased to **€58/month** in 2025
- Valid for **all local and regional public transport** nationwide
- Covers: Regional trains (RE, RB), S-Bahn, U-Bahn, trams, buses
- Does **NOT** cover: ICE, IC/EC, FlixBus, FlixTrain
- Monthly subscription, cancellable monthly
- Available as a digital ticket (app or chipcard)

### BahnCard (Deutsche Bahn Discount Cards)
- **BahnCard 25**: 25% discount on all DB tickets — costs €59.90/year (2nd class)
- **BahnCard 50**: 50% discount — costs €244/year (2nd class)
- **BahnCard 100**: Unlimited travel on all DB trains — costs €4,550/year (2nd class)

## Rules and Etiquette

### Ticket Validation
- Some cities require **validating (entwerten)** your ticket before boarding by stamping it at a machine on the platform
- Electronic tickets (apps) are pre-validated
- Ticket inspectors (Kontrolleure/Prüfdienst) conduct random checks

### Fare Evasion (Schwarzfahren)
- Traveling without a valid ticket is called **"Erschleichen von Leistungen"** (obtaining services by deception)
- **Erhöhtes Beförderungsentgelt**: Penalty of **€60** (as of 2024)
- Repeated offenses can be reported to the police and prosecuted criminally
- Criminal penalty: fine or up to **1 year imprisonment**

### Behavior Rules
- Priority seats (marked) must be given up for elderly, disabled, and pregnant passengers
- Eating and drinking: Generally tolerated on long-distance trains, but prohibited or discouraged on local transport in some cities
- Music must be listened to with headphones
- Smoking is prohibited in all vehicles and most stations
- Bicycles: Allowed on most trains with a bicycle ticket; rush-hour restrictions may apply
- Dogs: Small dogs in carriers ride free; larger dogs need a reduced-price ticket
- Strollers and wheelchairs have priority in designated areas

## Accessibility
- Most newer stations are barrier-free (step-free access)
- Older stations may lack elevators — DB provides accessibility information in its app
- Audio announcements and visual displays are standard on most services
- Wheelchair spaces are available on all long-distance and most regional trains
- Assistance service: Book 48 hours in advance through DB Mobilitätsservice

## Night Transport (Nachtverkehr)
- Many cities operate night buses (Nachtbus) and night trams
- S-Bahn and U-Bahn run 24/7 on weekends in major cities (Berlin, Hamburg, Munich)
- Weeknight service: Typically until 00:30–01:00, resuming at 04:00–05:00
- Night surcharges may apply on some services
