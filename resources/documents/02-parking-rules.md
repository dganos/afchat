# Parking Rules in Germany

## General Parking Regulations

Parking in Germany is regulated by §12 of the StVO (Straßenverkehrs-Ordnung).

### Definitions
- **Halten (Stopping)**: Voluntarily bringing the vehicle to a standstill for up to 3 minutes, or while the driver remains with the vehicle
- **Parken (Parking)**: Leaving the vehicle stationary for more than 3 minutes without the driver being present

## Where Parking is Prohibited

Parking is forbidden in the following areas:
- Within **5 meters** of intersections and junctions
- Within **10 meters** of traffic lights (if the vehicle would obstruct the view)
- Within **15 meters** of bus or tram stops (measured from the stop sign)
- On **priority roads** outside urban areas
- In front of **driveways and garage entrances**
- On **bike lanes** and designated cycling infrastructure
- On **narrow roads** where less than 3 meters of clearance remains for passing traffic
- On roads with a **continuous center line** if clearance is insufficient
- Within **5 meters** before and after pedestrian crossings (Zebrastreifen)
- On **sidewalks** (unless explicitly permitted by sign 315)

## Parking Zones and Systems

### Parkschein (Pay-and-display)
- Purchase a ticket from a Parkscheinautomat (parking meter)
- Display the ticket visibly on the dashboard
- Typical costs: €0.50–€3.00 per hour depending on the city

### Parkscheibe (Parking disc)
- Required in blue-marked parking zones (Blaue Zone)
- Set the disc to the next half-hour after arrival
- Maximum parking duration is posted on signs (commonly 1–2 hours)
- Discs must conform to standard design (blue and white, 11 cm × 15 cm)

### Bewohnerparken (Resident parking)
- Designated zones for residents with permits
- Permits cost €10–€30 per year (varies by city; some cities now charge up to €360/year)
- Non-residents may park for limited time with a Parkschein

### Parking Garages (Parkhäuser)
- Widely available in city centers
- Costs range from €1–€5 per hour
- Many offer daily maximum rates of €15–€30

## Special Rules
- **Disabled parking**: Blue badge holders may use designated disabled spaces; the badge must be displayed
- **Electric vehicles**: May park free at charging stations while charging; some cities offer free parking for EVs
- **Winter**: Vehicles must not be parked where they obstruct snow removal operations
