# Driver's License in Germany

## License Classes

Germany follows the EU driver's license classification system.

### Passenger Vehicle Classes
| Class | Description | Minimum Age |
|---|---|---|
| AM | Mopeds up to 45 km/h, up to 50cc | 15 (16 in some states) |
| A1 | Motorcycles up to 125cc, 11 kW | 16 |
| A2 | Motorcycles up to 35 kW | 18 |
| A | All motorcycles (unrestricted) | 24 (or 20 with 2 years A2) |
| B | Cars up to 3,500 kg, up to 8 passenger seats | 18 (17 with BF17) |
| BE | Class B + trailer over 750 kg (combined up to 7,000 kg) | 18 |

### Commercial Vehicle Classes
| Class | Description | Minimum Age |
|---|---|---|
| C1 | Trucks 3,500–7,500 kg | 18 |
| C | Trucks over 3,500 kg (no upper limit) | 21 |
| D1 | Buses up to 16 passenger seats | 21 |
| D | Buses with more than 16 passenger seats | 24 |

## Obtaining a License (Class B)

### Steps
1. **First Aid Course (Erste-Hilfe-Kurs)**: 9 teaching units, costs ~€30–€50
2. **Eye Test (Sehtest)**: Conducted by an optician, costs ~€6–€7
3. **Passport Photos**: Biometric photos required
4. **Register at a Fahrschule (Driving School)**: Mandatory in Germany — self-study is not permitted
5. **Theory Lessons**: Minimum 12 basic lessons + 2 additional for Class B (each 90 minutes)
6. **Practical Lessons**: Minimum 12 special driving lessons:
   - 5 on rural roads (Überlandfahrten)
   - 4 on the Autobahn
   - 3 at night (Beleuchtungsfahrten)
   - Plus regular practice lessons (number varies by student ability)
7. **Theory Exam**: 30 multiple-choice questions, max 10 fault points to pass
8. **Practical Exam**: 55 minutes minimum, conducted by a TÜV/DEKRA examiner

### Costs
Obtaining a Class B license typically costs **€2,000–€3,500** in total:
- Driving school fees: €200–€400
- Learning materials: €50–€100
- Theory lessons: included in school fees
- Practical lessons: €40–€70 per 45-minute session
- Theory exam: ~€25
- Practical exam: ~€120
- First aid course: €30–€50
- Eye test + photos: ~€20

## Probationary Period (Probezeit)

- Duration: **2 years** from the date of obtaining the first license
- During this period:
  - **Zero alcohol tolerance (0.0‰ BAC)**
  - Serious violations (A-Verstöße) result in:
    - Extension of probation to **4 years**
    - Mandatory participation in an **advanced seminar (Aufbauseminar)**
  - Two A-violations or one A-violation + two B-violations → license revocation

## Begleitetes Fahren (BF17) — Accompanied Driving
- Available from age **17**
- Must be accompanied by a designated person who:
  - Is at least 30 years old
  - Has held a license for at least 5 years
  - Has fewer than 1 point in Flensburg
  - BAC under 0.5‰ while accompanying
- At age 18, restrictions are lifted automatically

## Foreign License Holders
- EU/EEA licenses: **Valid indefinitely** in Germany, no conversion needed
- Non-EU licenses: Valid for **6 months** after establishing residence; must then convert
- Conversion process varies by country — some require only an eye test, others require both exams
- Countries with conversion agreements include: USA (some states), Canada, Japan, South Korea, Australia
